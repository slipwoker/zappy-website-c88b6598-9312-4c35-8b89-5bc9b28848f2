Our Philosophy

Where Raw Wood Becomes a Living Keepsake

Every piece we create begins with a conversation between material and maker. We work exclusively with olive and acacia — two woods that carry centuries of warmth, grain, and story in every ring. No two slabs are alike, and we honor that truth rather than sand it away.

Epoxy resin enters our process not as a shortcut, but as an artistic companion — a way to preserve the natural voids, cracks, and edges that give each board or mezuzah its singular character. The result is a handmade craft that bridges functional beauty with spiritual meaning: cutting boards that grace the kitchen table, and mezuzahs that mark the threshold of a home with intention.

This is shopping handmade in its truest sense — each object touched, shaped, and finished by a single pair of hands, made for those who believe that the things we live with should carry genuine soul.

From Forest to Finish

The Making of Every Piece

Each creation from our handmade craft shop begins with patience — a quiet, deliberate journey through four essential stages that transform raw natural materials into functional art.

01

Selecting the Slab

Every piece begins with a single, deliberate choice. We source our slabs of olive wood and acacia directly from trusted growers, hand-selecting each board for its grain character, natural edge integrity, and structural soundness. No two slabs are alike — the swirling patterns, knots, and color variations are what make each finished piece entirely one of a kind. We look for material that tells a story before a single cut is made.

02

Planning the Resin Pour

Before a drop of epoxy resin is mixed, we spend time with each slab — studying its voids, natural cracks, and live edges to determine where the river of resin will flow most beautifully. The resin colour is chosen with intention: our signature river-blue tones echo the organic meeting of water and earth. Pigment ratios are measured precisely, and the mould is set to guide the pour along the wood's natural contours. This planning stage is where artistry meets material science.

03

Shaping & Finishing

Once the resin has cured fully — a process we never rush — the real sculpting begins. We move through progressive grits of hand-sanding, starting coarse to level the surface and refining down to ultra-fine grits that reveal the wood's true luminosity. The boundary where grain meets resin is coaxed smooth until it becomes seamless to the touch. Edges are shaped and softened by hand, whether left organically raw or refined into clean lines, depending on the piece's intended character.

04

Final Sealing & Inspection

The final stage is where each piece earns its place in your home. Food-safe oils and protective sealants are applied by hand in multiple coats, enhancing the wood's natural warmth and locking in the resin's depth. We inspect every surface in raking light — checking for imperfections, ensuring the finish is flawless, and confirming that the piece meets our standard for handmade gifts worth keeping for a lifetime. Only then is it signed, wrapped, and ready to leave the studio.

Our Materials

The Wood Beneath the Art

Every piece begins with a choice of wood — a choice that shapes texture, warmth, and story. We work exclusively with two noble species, each selected for its singular character and enduring beauty.

01

Olive Wood

Olea europaea

Olive wood is among the most storied timbers in the world — grown slowly over decades in Mediterranean groves, its grain is a living record of patience. The wood reveals swirling, unpredictable patterns of cream, honey, and deep amber that no two pieces share alike. Its fine, dense texture takes polish beautifully, rewarding the hand with a silken warmth that only deepens with use. In our handmade craft pieces, olive wood brings a sense of ancient rootedness — a material that carries cultural memory and spiritual resonance in every ring.

  • Intricate swirling grain
  • Honey, cream & amber tones
  • Dense, fine-textured hardwood
  • Deeply polishable surface
02

Acacia Wood

Acacia spp.

Acacia commands attention through strength and warmth. Its grain runs bold and direct — rich caramel and walnut-brown tones layered with natural variation that speaks of resilience and vitality. One of the hardest commercially available timbers, acacia resists wear and moisture with quiet confidence, making it an ideal foundation for functional pieces that live in daily life. Its open-grain texture creates a tactile richness that contrasts beautifully with polished resin, and its warmth grounds every piece with an earthy, generous character that feels both modern and timeless.

  • Bold, directional grain
  • Caramel, walnut & gold tones
  • Exceptionally hard & durable
  • Moisture-resistant surface

Blue Resin — The River Within

The epoxy resin we use is chosen not merely as a filler, but as a co-material — a living element that brings movement and light into every piece. Our signature blue resin ranges from deep sapphire to translucent cobalt, shifting in tone as light passes through it at different angles. Where wood offers warmth and grain, resin offers depth and luminosity — a river-like flow that winds through the natural landscape of the timber rather than concealing it.

The translucency is intentional. Beneath the surface, you can trace the wood grain continuing beneath the resin, creating a layered visual depth that rewards close looking. The contrast is not a conflict — it is a conversation between the organic and the crystalline, the ancient and the contemporary. In our handmade gifts and cutting boards, this blue accent brings a modern edge that speaks to design-conscious homes while preserving the soul of the natural material beneath.
