Olive Wood & Epoxy Resin 

The Fusion of Nature and Art: Olive Wood and Epoxy Resin 
Olive wood and epoxy resin products represent perfect harmony between ancient tradition and modern innovation. 
By combining the raw and organic beauty of centuries-old olive trees with the smooth and versatile nature of quality resin, we create unique handcrafted artworks that are both durable and stunning with distinctive aesthetics. 
Since no two olive wood boards are identical, each item - from cutting boards to mezuzahs and art pieces - 
is a unique masterpiece. 
The resin integrates with the "wild edges" 
(the natural line) of the wood, ensuring that no two pieces will ever be identical.

Our Craft

Where Nature Meets Artistry

 Every creation we make begins with respect for carefully selected natural materials, 


designed and high-quality. This is craftsmanship at its finest.

Natural and Selected Hardwoods 

We use only the finest olive and acacia wood — each plank is selected for its unique structural character, natural color variation, and long-term durability. No two creations are identical, because no two trees are the same.

Artistic Resin

Our special epoxy resin is hand-poured into the natural wood channels, creating luminous veins of color that reflect flowing water. Each pour is carefully timed, layered and cured to achieve a smooth glass-like finish that is food-safe, child-safe and built to last for generations.

Quality handcrafted finish 

Every edge is hand-smoothed gradually. Every surface is oiled, polished and inspected before it leaves our workshop. From natural cutting boards to handcrafted mezuzahs, the finish is where intention becomes creation.

Signature Works

Each Piece Tells the Story of Its Wood

No two pieces from Natural Wood Art are alike. The grain movement of olive and acacia, the organic flow of blue epoxy resin rivers, and the quiet decisions made during hand-finishing — together they make every cutting board, mezuzah, and decorative object a singular artifact. These are not mass-produced goods; they are handmade gifts shaped by the material itself.
